Arsenal vs Man City: A Balanced View on the Carabao Cup Final

By The Armoury 21 March 2026 5 min read Opinion

It’s cup final day tomorrow. Arsenal against Manchester City at Wembley. The two best teams in the country stepping away from the relentless grind of the Premier League title race to contest the first major silverware of the season.

Going into this one, I’m feeling surprisingly balanced. There’s a lot of noise surrounding the fixture — City’s recent wobble, Arsenal’s momentum, the history between Arteta and Guardiola — but the reality is that this match isn’t going to have a huge impact on either club’s season, provided the result isn’t a heavy defeat. It’s a final, yes, but the real war is being fought in the league.

The Stakes and the History

There’s a narrative floating around that Mikel Arteta is under pressure to deliver a trophy. Let’s correct the record: he won the FA Cup in his first season and has two Community Shields to his name. What he hasn’t won is the League Cup. In fact, Arsenal haven’t won this particular competition since 1993. I’ve been a fan since 1994, so seeing us lift this specific trophy would actually be a novelty.

Winning it would be a great bonus, especially against a side of City’s quality, but it’s a low-pressure situation. If we win, it’s a brilliant day out and a psychological boost. If we lose a tight game, we dust ourselves off and focus on the league and Europe.

The Lineup Dilemma: Raya or Kepa?

The biggest talking point heading into the match is who starts in goal. Kepa Arrizabalaga has been Arteta’s designated cup goalkeeper this season, and he hasn’t put a foot wrong. The expectation from many quarters is that Arteta will honour that contribution and start him at Wembley.

I disagree. With absolutely no disrespect to Kepa, we have to start David Raya. When you are playing Manchester City in a cup final, you play your best team. Raya’s distribution, his command of the box, and his understanding with the back four are fundamental to how we play. In a game of fine margins, you don’t leave your number one on the bench.

Where the Game Will Be Won

When I look at where this game will be decided, my eyes are drawn to the physical battles. I think Declan Rice is going to be absolutely massive for us in the middle of the park. His ability to break up play and drive us forward will dictate the tempo.

Defensively, Big Gabi (Gabriel Magalhães) and Ben White are going to be the difference-makers. They need to be flawless. On the flip side, the player who worries me most from City isn’t necessarily Erling Haaland — who famously hasn’t scored in a final for them yet — but Rayan Cherki. His unpredictability and technical ability in tight spaces can unlock even the most disciplined defences.

The Verdict

Ultimately, what I want to see tomorrow is two top-class teams playing exciting, positive football. No dark arts, no cynicism — just a proper game of football between the two best sides in England.

I think we have the edge right now. City are vulnerable, and we are playing with a quiet, ruthless confidence. I’m backing Arsenal to win it 2-1.

Up the Gunners.
