# The Armoury — Author Style Guide

> This file captures the voice, tone, and preferences of the blog's author for use in all future posts.

---

## Voice & Tone

- **Balanced and measured** — not reactionary or hyperbolic. Avoids extreme takes.
- **Fan perspective, not pundit** — writes from lived experience as a supporter since 1994, not as a detached analyst.
- **Honest and direct** — states opinions clearly without hedging excessively, but acknowledges nuance.
- **Respectful of opponents** — gives credit where it's due; no cheap digs or tribalism for its own sake.
- **Passionate but grounded** — excited about the club, but keeps perspective on what actually matters.

---

## Writing Style

- Short, punchy paragraphs. No waffle.
- Opinions stated plainly: *"I think..."*, *"I disagree..."*, *"We have to..."*
- Uses nicknames naturally: *"Big Gabi"*, *"Rice"*, *"Kepa"* — first name or surname, rarely full name in body text.
- Tactical observations are practical, not overly technical — focused on impact and matchups, not formations.
- Ends posts with a clear verdict or a rallying line. Often short and punchy: *"Up the Gunners."*

---

## Recurring Themes & Opinions

- **Raya over Kepa** — believes the best XI should always play, no rotation for sentiment.
- **Rice is the engine** — consistently highlights Declan Rice as the key player.
- **Defensive solidity matters** — Gabriel Magalhães and Ben White are frequently cited as difference-makers.
- **Arteta context** — acknowledges his FA Cup and Community Shield wins; doesn't pile on unfairly.
- **Trophy perspective** — the Carabao Cup is a novelty/bonus; the league and Champions League are the real prizes.
- **Football philosophy** — wants positive, attacking football from both sides. Dislikes cynicism and "dark arts".

---

## Post Structure (Preferred Format)

1. **Opening paragraph** — sets the scene, no fluff, straight into the topic.
2. **Section with `<h2>` headers** — 3–4 sections, each with a clear focus.
3. **One key opinion section** — where the author's voice is strongest (e.g. lineup debate).
4. **The Verdict** — short, decisive closing section with a score prediction or clear conclusion.
5. **Sign-off line** — brief, fan-voice closer. e.g. *"Up the Gunners."*

---

## Post Metadata Defaults

- **Author name:** The Armoury
- **Category:** Opinion (for previews/analysis), Match Reports (for post-match)
- **Tags:** Always include the competition, both clubs, and post type (e.g. Match Preview, Match Report, Opinion)
- **Reading time:** Aim for 5–8 min reads (800–1200 words)

---

## Banner Image Style

- **Photorealistic, cinematic** — no cartoons or flat graphics.
- **Player-focused** — specific named players in action or face-off compositions.
- **Wembley / stadium atmosphere** — floodlit nights, crowd in background, dramatic lighting.
- **Kit-accurate** — Arsenal red/white, opponent in correct colours.
- **16:9 aspect ratio** for all hero banners.
